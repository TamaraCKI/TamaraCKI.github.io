About me

I would like to share my enthusiasm and wonder about the world, with anyone who is open to it! That's why I took the Science Communication Specialization in Leiden. As part of that, I did an internship at New Scientist.

With a BSc Artificial Intelligence degree in my pocket, I was able to sink my teeth into the MSc Computing Science in Utrecht. There I encountered really difficult problems. What I learned: think, think more, and don't give up until the solution is in sight.

In my master's thesis, I developed geometric algorithms applied to plant seed dispersion, with Frank Staals and Sarita de Berg as supervisors and Marc van Kreveld as second proofreader. My thesis can be read here.

I am currently doing a PhD at CWI and Utrecht University under the supervision of Tim Baarslag and Pinar Yolum. The following years, I try to teach computers how to negotiate.
